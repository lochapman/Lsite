The Dungeons and Dragons alignment system works like this:

Lawful good
Neutral good
Chaotic good
Lawful neutral
True neutral
Chaotic neutral
Lawful evil
Neutral evil
Chaotic evil

These alignments are how morality is determined in D&D, though the definition of 'evil' and 'good' varies, since 'good' is defined as prioritizing the needs of others first, and 'evil' is defined as prioritizing the needs of yourself first.

As an example; a Hobgoblin is Lawful evil because of their glory seeking ideals and rigid societal norms, while a Storm giant is chaotic good because of their views of responsibility and non-recognition of higher authority
